Reactable Wall — Phase 3, as projected on the wall

A full patch: oscillator → filter → delay → output, an LFO modulating the filter, and the Tonality puck snapping pitch to a scale. This is a mockup of the projected visuals, not a screenshot.

Oscillator — the sound source. Turn it to set pitch.
Filter — spliced onto the cable. Turn it for cutoff.
Delay — next on the cable. Turn it for feedback.
Output — the speaker. Turn it for master volume.
LFO — modulates the nearest puck (here, the filter).
Tonality — global; snaps pitch to a scale.
Audio cable — green, glowing, flows toward Output.
Control link — purple, dotted; carries modulation, not sound.
How you'd play this: Place the OSC and OUT pucks on the wall — a green cable snaps between them and you hear a tone. Drop the FILT puck on that cable and it splices into the chain (osc → filter → out); slide the DLY puck onto the cable too and it chains after the filter. Where each effect sits along the line decides its order. Set the LFO puck near the filter and a purple control link forms — the filter's cutoff now wobbles at the LFO's rate (you chose B: the filter's own rotation still sets the center cutoff, the LFO sets how fast it wobbles). The TON puck anywhere on the wall keeps the oscillator in key. Turning any puck shows its parameter as the bright arc around its ring.
